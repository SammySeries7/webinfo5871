Introduction


Electric vehicles (EVs) are vehicles that are powered by electricity from batteries or fuel cells, rather than gasoline or diesel. The concept of an electric vehicle was introduced in the early 1990s. However, the technology could not become practical at that time. Nowadays, the technology in both battery, motors, and chipsets have become more advanced enough to produce the vehicle in the range of 300-350 miles per one charge, which allows many people to commute between their home and their nearby destinations. Today, there are several types of electric vehicles, including all-electric vehicles (EVs), hybrid electric vehicles (HEVs), and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs). These vehicles have become increasingly popular in recent years due to their potential to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and decrease emissions.



The EV type

“Do you prefer to use EVs?” is the main idea in this text analysis project. After text data from various sources were gathered, there are evidently two sides, which are people that support buying and using the vehicle and people who tend to stick with gas-powered vehicles. Regarding the supported people, they love the way the EVs produce zero emissions while in operation. This helps to reduce air pollution and improve public health. Air pollution has been linked to a wide range of health problems, including respiratory issues, heart disease, and stroke. By reducing the number of emissions-producing vehicles on the road, EVs can help to improve air quality and reduce the health impacts of air pollution. In addition, the EVs lover thinks that EVs can also help to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and decrease the greenhouse gas. This means the vehicle emits less greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and carbon monoxide, which can help relieve the global warming issue.


For the opposers to EVs, they believe that the production of EVs and their batteries require the mining of rare earth metals such as lithium, which have a more detrimental effect on the environment. Some components in the battery cannot be recycled and are a risk to people's health. Another point is the lack of infrastructure. Many people are likely to use an EV as their primary cars which are required to use in daily life and road trips. The problem of the lack of charging points caused them not to be able to use them on their road trip. Some consumers do not have a charging area and charging facility on their own. This makes people feel difficult to use an EV on a daily basis. In addition, the range of battery is not enough if the EVs are used with the freeway speed. The batteries tend to reduce very fast. Finally, the cost of EVs is still relatively high compared to traditional gasoline-powered vehicles, which can make them less accessible to some consumers.



The traffic in a big city

In the US, many reports indicate that 91.55% of households will have access to at least one vehicle in 2020, up from 90.82% in 2015. The estimated number of cars is around 270 million in 2020, up from 260 million in 2015. This is just for only a country. If we imagine how many cars there are in the world, the number may be more than 20 times the number of cars in the US. The more cars we have, the more air pollution will exist. Household combustion vehicles are common sources of air pollution. Pollutants of major public health concern include particulate matter, carbon monoxide, ozone, nitrogen dioxide and sulfur dioxide. Outdoor and indoor air pollution cause respiratory and other diseases and are important sources of morbidity and mortality. In addition, the combustion of the vehicle can cause fine particulate matter (PM2.5) in the air. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) is an air pollutant that is a concern for people's health when levels in air are high. PM2.5 are tiny particles in the air that reduce visibility and cause the air to appear hazy when levels are elevated. Previous epidemiological studies have indicated that ambient PM2.5 is carcinogenic, and may increase the morbidity and mortality rates associated with lung cancer. Therefore, renewable energy cars such as EVs may be the solution to help solve this situation in the long term plan because they can help limit the air pollution into the environment.

In addition to concerns about air pollution, this report aims to deliver more about the problems of EVs that have impacts on both the environment and consumers. There are problems with the infrastructure network, EVs’ cost and the battery components as mentioned in the previous paragraph. Sustainable development is the key for product development. The idea is to provide necessary resources and ecosystem services to humans without the environmental damages. The major issues can be solved if all EV’s manufacturers are concerned about the issues and feel determined to solve them. Besides, all countries have to find enough energy sources for the vehicle. Currently, most countries use fossil fuels to produce electricity, which does not fit with the purpose of the EVs. Many more research in the material sciences to improve solar cells and battery efficiency should be considered a priority. The sunlight is the free and clean energy source. If there is a way to produce more electricity from sunlight and its eco-friendly storage, many opponents may turn into supporters in the future.

Both news and users’ opinions are collected from Reddit, Twitter, NewsAPI, CNBC and InsideEVs. The first two sources are about users’ opinion and discussion. NewsAPI, CNBC and InsideEvs are about the news and technology in EVs. Reddit, Twitter, NewsAPI are collected via the APIs, whereas the two latter sources are from web scraping. After all data have already been collected, they are classified into five labels according to the problems of EVs in the previous paragraphs. The labels are environment, news, battery, infrastructure, and cost. The label of environment talks about the impact of EVs on the environment. The label of news explains about the information and technology in EVs. The label of battery is about the battery capacity, charging time, and the battery’s efficiency. The label of infrastructure is about the charging networks and its facilities. The last one is about the cost of the EVs and electricity fuels in different locations. All special characters are removed from the text data and transformed to lemmas by lemmatization and stemming techniques. After that , the data are used to create a bag of words by the CounterVectorizer package, which helps to remove the sequences between terms in documents. However, CounterVectorizer cannot be used to measure the importance of terms among all documents. Term Frequency - Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) is employed to measure how important a term is within a document relative to a collection of documents.
