Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)


What Topic Modelling is and how to use it to gain information


Topic modelling is a technique which assigns topic to a given corpus based on the words present. Topic modelling is important, because in this world full of data it has become increasingly important to categories the documents. For example, a company receives hundreds of reviews, then it is important for the company to know what categories of reviews are more important and vice versa. Topics can be thought of as keywords which can describe a document, for example, for a topic sports the words that come to our mind our volleyball, basketball, tennis, cricket etc. A topic model is a model, which can automatically detect topics based on the words appearing in a document. In this section, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) will be used as the topic modelling.


Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) is a generative probabilistic model for topic modeling. LDA assumes that documents are composed of a mixture of topics, where a topic is a distribution over a fixed set of words. Each document is generated by, first, sampling a distribution over topics from a Dirichlet distribution, sampling a topic from the previously sampled distribution over topics for each word in the document, and sampling a word from the topic accordingly. The model is unsupervised, which means it learns the topics from the data without any prior knowledge about the data. The algorithm iteratively updates the topic distribution and word distribution until convergence, using a technique called Gibbs sampling.


The method is used to cluster the texts following their abstract topics. Several clusters will be tested in the LDA model to assess how well each topic is perceived by using coherence measures. After that, the topic definition will be interpreted from the terms in each topic, and the model can also be assigned the topic for each document following the topic distribution. In addition, after the suitable number of clusters is selected, LDA helps visualize how close the topics in documents are by using the principal component analysis.

Results and Discussion


coherence score c_v

The suitable number of clusters is the first object to check. The coherence score from the genism package is used for scoring each number of clusters. The coherence measure represents the “quality human perception” about topics in unique, objective, and easy-to-evaluate numbers. The topic coherence metric assesses how well a topic is supported by a text set, which is called reference corpus. It uses statistics and probabilities drawn from the reference corpus, especially focused on the word’s context, to give a coherence score to a topic. According to the picture above, the appropriate numbers of clusters are 3, 4, and 5 respectively. Three is a suitable number for clustering, which is confirmed by scores in the above figure.


3-cluster model


The first topic

In the 3-cluster model, the first topic contains the battery properties and attributes terms such as “range,” “mile,” “charging station,” “home,” “afford,” and “time”. With these words, it is implicitly interpreted that consumers are concerned with the limited range of EVs. The long charging time and limited charging station are what they worry about. The price of EVs is unreasonably high compared to gas-powered cars. An EV, also, needs a garage to charge a battery during the night.


The second topic

The second topic is all about Elon Musk and Tesla. The outstanding words are “tesla”, “musk”, “elon”, “model”, “stock” and “price”. These words indicate the role of Elon Musk on the EVs’ society. Elon musk is Tesla's CEO. He often says and guides the direction of EVs, which often has an impact on Tesla's stock price. His products such as model-3, model-x, and model-y often get accused of being too expensive.


The third topic

The third topic is the impact of batteries on the environment. The outstanding words are “environment”, “lithium”, “fossil”, “recycle” and “mining”. EVs’ batteries are made of lithium which comes from mining. Lithium mining has lots of impact on the environment. Batteries are very hard to recycle because they are made of complex components and rare earth minerals. Regarding power sources for EVs, electricity in the United States largely comes from the fossil fuel generator, which causes greenhouse gas and air pollution. In the case of the 5-cluster model, the coherence measure gives the score around 0.48, whereas the 3-cluster coherence measure gives the score around 0.54. The difference in the score causes ambiguous EVs’ topics in the former model. According to the first two figures, the second cluster and the fifth cluster seem to have similar terms in their topics. The second can be assumed that it is related to Elon’s activities in the EVs’ society. However, the fifth combines many subtopics, which cause the ambiguous interpretation of meaning. According to the last figure, other topics are subtle differences from the 3-cluster model. The first may be about charging stations and batteries. The second is about the environmental issues from battery production. The third and the fourth are the problems mentioned earlier. The last one is about models and other brands in the EVs’ industry.

Conclusion

Latent Dirichlet Allocation gives the most clear understanding among the 3 clusters. The topics are the battery properties and attributes, Elon Musk and Tesla, and environmental impacts from EVs. The coherent measure plays an important role to help select the appropriate number of clusters in the model. The 3-cluster model’s score is 0.54, whereas the 5-cluster is about 0.48. The higher score makes the topic easier to understand. The 5-cluster model cannot properly differentiate the topic of Elon musk from the other topics.
